# Legal Asset Holding

## Overview

All collective assets, funds, and property belonging to the Social Production community are held by an international non-profit foundation established solely to steward community-owned resources. The foundation has no shareholders and no profit motive. It exists only to provide legal standing for collective ownership — it does not govern, it does not decide.

The community governs through the platform. The foundation executes what the community decides.

Funding a project gives a contributor no additional governance authority or ownership claim. Contributions go to the collective. The foundation holds everything in trust for the entire community.

---

## Founding Principles

The foundation's founding documents legally bind it to:

- Hold all assets in trust for the Social Production user community
- Take no action with any asset or fund without verified on-chain community consent
- Maintain full public transparency of all holdings, transactions, and fund movements via the blockchain

---

## Blockchain as Authority

The blockchain is the authoritative record of all community decisions. Any action taken by the foundation without verified on-chain consent constitutes a breach of fiduciary duty. Directors are legally bound to execute only what the blockchain records as community decisions. The foundation does not interpret, override, or substitute its own judgment for the community's.

---

## Assets Held

The foundation may hold:

- Bank accounts and financial assets
- Real property and equipment
- Intellectual property
- Digital assets — cryptocurrency, domain names, and similar

No asset is owned by any individual. All assets are held in trust for the community. Assets produced by projects belong to the collective by default — the materials used in production, the outputs, and the infrastructure supporting them all sit under the foundation's stewardship on the community's behalf.

---

## Funding and Escrow

Community funding for projects is held in escrow by the foundation until the project goal is met. No individual member holds funds at any point. The foundation functions as the community's bank — holding donations in escrow against a defined goal before releasing them for the project's use.

If a project fails or is cancelled before completion, committed funds are returned to contributors. All fund movements are recorded on-chain and publicly visible.

---

## Inter-Project Resource Flows

There are no separate project treasuries. All assets and resources sit under a single holding structure. When one project produces materials that another project needs, this is not an exchange or a sale between separate entities — it is a movement of collectively held resources, authorised by community governance and recorded on-chain as a transaction.

Project creators and managers register produced quantities in the platform database. The blockchain records that a distribution or transfer event was authorised and executed. Receiving parties confirm receipt on-chain. The full lifecycle from production through distribution is tracked transparently in both systems — the database holds the operational detail, the blockchain holds the authorisation record.

---

## Dissolution

In the event of dissolution, all assets held by the foundation must transfer to another organisation with an equivalent mission and principles. Assets may never be distributed to individuals and may never be transferred to for-profit entities under any circumstances. This clause exists to ensure that community-held resources cannot be captured by private interests even in the event of the foundation ceasing to operate.

---

## Immutability of Core Principles

The foundational constraints of the foundation — asset stewardship, community consent, transparency, and director constraints — cannot be amended through standard governance processes. Any change to these core principles requires an extraordinary community vote meeting a significantly higher threshold than routine governance decisions. This threshold is defined in the founding documents and protects the community from voting away its own structural protections.

---

## Director Appointment and Removal

Foundation directors are appointed and removed solely through verified on-chain community votes. Directors have no independent authority. Their role is to execute community decisions within the legal framework of the foundation's jurisdiction — nothing more. Director positions are not permanent and are subject to the same governance processes as any other platform decision.

---

## Jurisdiction

Jurisdiction has not yet been decided. This is a significant decision that affects what assets the foundation can hold, director liability, and tax status. Options to research with legal counsel include Switzerland, the Netherlands (Stichting structure), Estonia, and other jurisdictions with established non-profit foundation law. Estonia is worth consideration given its advanced digital governance frameworks.

This will be finalised once the governance and operational model is sufficiently developed to demonstrate clearly to legal counsel how the foundation's relationship to on-chain governance works in practice.
