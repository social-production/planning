# Legal

This document is the current working draft for Social Production's legal structure and asset stewardship model.

## Overview

All collective assets, funds, and property belonging to the Social Production community are held by an international non-profit foundation established solely to steward community-owned resources. The foundation has no shareholders and no profit motive. It exists only to provide legal standing for collective ownership. It does not govern and it does not decide.

The community governs through the platform. The foundation executes what the community decides.

Funding a project gives a contributor no additional governance authority or ownership claim. Contributions go to the collective. The foundation holds everything in trust for the entire community.

## Founding Principles

The foundation's founding documents legally bind it to:

- Hold all assets in trust for the Social Production user community
- Take no action with any asset or fund without verified on-chain community consent
- Maintain full public transparency of all holdings, transactions, and fund movements via the blockchain

## Blockchain as Authority

The blockchain is the authoritative record of all community decisions. Any action taken by the foundation without verified on-chain consent constitutes a breach of fiduciary duty. Directors are legally bound to execute only what the blockchain records as community decisions. The foundation does not interpret, override, or substitute its own judgment for the community's.

## Assets Held

The foundation may hold:

- Bank accounts and financial assets
- Real property and equipment
- Intellectual property

No asset is owned by any individual. All assets are held in trust for the community. Assets produced by projects belong to the collective by default. Materials, outputs, and supporting infrastructure all sit under the foundation's stewardship on the community's behalf.

## Funding and Escrow

Community funding for projects is held in escrow by the foundation until the project goal is met. No individual member holds funds at any point. The foundation functions as the community's bank, holding contributions against a defined goal before releasing them for project use.

If a project fails or is canceled before completion, committed funds are returned to contributors. All fund movements are recorded and publicly visible.

## Inter-Project Resource Flows

There are no separate project treasuries. All assets and resources sit under a single holding structure. When one project produces materials that another project needs, this is not an exchange or a sale between separate entities. It is a movement of collectively held resources authorized by community governance and recorded as a transaction.

Project creators and managers register produced quantities in the platform database. The blockchain records that a transfer event was authorized and executed. Receiving parties confirm receipt. The database holds operational detail and the blockchain holds the authorization record.

## Dissolution

In the event of dissolution, all assets held by the foundation must transfer to another organization with an equivalent mission and principles. Assets may never be distributed to individuals and may never be transferred to for-profit entities.

This clause exists to prevent community-held resources from being captured by private interests if the foundation ceases to operate.

## Immutability of Core Principles

The foundation's core constraints, including asset stewardship, community consent, transparency, and limits on director authority, cannot be amended through standard governance processes. Any change to these core principles requires an extraordinary community vote with a significantly higher threshold than routine governance decisions.

## Director Appointment and Removal

Foundation directors are appointed and removed solely through verified on-chain community votes. Directors have no independent authority. Their role is to execute community decisions within the legal framework of the foundation's jurisdiction and nothing more.

## Jurisdiction

Jurisdiction has not yet been decided. This is a significant decision because it affects asset holding, director liability, and tax status. Options to research with legal counsel include Switzerland, the Netherlands using a Stichting structure, Estonia, and other jurisdictions with established non-profit foundation law.

This will be finalized once the governance and operational model is developed enough to demonstrate clearly how the foundation relates to on-chain governance in practice.

## Status

This is a planning document. Legal structure, jurisdiction, and specific governance mechanisms will be finalized with appropriate legal counsel.
